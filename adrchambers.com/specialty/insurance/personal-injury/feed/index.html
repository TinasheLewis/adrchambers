Bill has over 40 years of experience in labour relations and human resources. As a human resources executive, Bill was responsible for conflict coaching, mentorship, and all HR-related activities for thousands of employees. Bill earned his B.A. (Economics) from Brown University in Providence, Rhode Island and his certificate in Dispute Resolution from York University. He is available to conduct mediations and investigations in-person, by video conference and by teleconference.

Wolf spent the past 14 years as a Superior Court Judge in London, Kingston and Belleville (Ontario) where he presided over trials involving medical malpractice, defamation, insurance, family, wills & estates, municipal and environmental disputes.  Prior to his appointment to the Superior Court, Wolf was a litigator at the firm of O’Flynn, Weese, Tausendfreund LLP in Belleville, where he practised civil and criminal litigation.

Wolf earned his LL.B. and B.A. at Western University in London, Ontario and attended alternative dispute resolution training with the Stitt Feld Handy Group.

Between 1985 and 2000, Wolf was a member of the Belleville City Council.  He was President of the Belleville YMCA, Belleville Rotary Club and Belleville Sales & Ad Club. Wolf started and coached the Belleville Swim Team and established the Belleville Bulldogs Rugby Club. He was a member of the National Board of YMCA and, in 2013, was awarded the YMCA Fellowship of Honour, the highest honour given by the YMCA for outstanding leadership.

Wolf resides in Belleville, Ontario and is willing to travel.

]]> Eban is a mediator, arbitrator and investigator with ADR Chambers.

Eban’s areas of expertise include human rights, wrongful dismissal, personal injury, motor vehicle accidents (SABS), workers’ compensation, occupational health and safety, labour relations, and First Nations disputes.

Eban brings over three decades of experience in alternative dispute resolution to his mediation, arbitration and investigation practice. His most recent post was as a Vice-Chair at the Human Rights Tribunal of Ontario where he conducted mediations, med-arbs and hearings involving diverse forms of discrimination. Eban spent 20 years as an Arbitrator at the Financial Services Commission of Ontario conducting pre-hearing conferences, mediations and arbitrations in no-fault automobile insurance and long-term disability claims. Eban was also a Vice-Chair at the Workplace Safety and Insurance Appeals Tribunal conducting hearings in workers’ compensation claims involving complex medical issues of causation, disability and rehabilitation. Between 1987 and 1995, Eban acted as counsel at the Workers’ Compensation Appeals Tribunal, Pay Equity Hearings Tribunal, and Ministry of Labour.

Eban earned his LL.M. (Administrative Law) at Osgoode Hall Law School, and his LL.B. at the University of Toronto Law School. He was called to the Bar in Toronto in 1987.

Eban is available to conduct mediations, arbitrations and investigations in-person, by video conference and teleconference.
